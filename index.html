Parameters

pₜ mode
ε(t) = 1/pₜ — boredom tracks error ε
s = +1 Improving: ε falls → may exit zone (mastery).
s = −1 Worsening: ε rises → may exit zone (failure).
s = 0   Constant: ε = 1/p₀, stays put in zone.
The comfort zone [ε_lo, ε_hi]
ε = 1/pₜ, so small ε means high mastery and large ε means low mastery. The zone is the range of challenge where k stays at its floor K₀ — no extra boredom penalty.
ε_lo is the mastery threshold: if ε drops below it (pₜ > 1/ε_lo), the task has become too easy and c_lo kicks in.
ε_hi is the failure threshold: if ε rises above it (pₜ < 1/ε_hi), the task is too hard and c_hi kicks in.
Narrowing the zone (ε_lo → ε_hi) means only a thin band of challenge feels comfortable. Widening it creates a large region where performance can wander without cost.
Wall shape as n increases
n=1 — V-shape: penalty is linear in distance from zone edge. Every extra unit outside costs the same; the wall rises at a constant rate.
n=2 — Smooth bowl (quadratic). Gentle near the zone boundary, then accelerates. Analogous to a spring — small deviations barely register, large ones feel heavy.
n=3 — Steep basin (cubic). Very flat just outside the zone — the agent barely notices crossing the edge — but punishment grows rapidly at larger deviations.
n=4 — Quartic: nearly-flat near the boundary, near-vertical far out. Like a hard wall with a wide buffer; the agent is almost indifferent until it wanders far, then hits a cliff.
Asymmetric gain coefficients
c_lo scales the penalty on the mastery side — when ε < ε_lo, meaning pₜ is high and the task has become too easy. Higher c_lo means the agent grows bored faster from over-competence.
c_hi scales the penalty on the failure side — when ε > ε_hi, meaning pₜ is low and the task is too hard. Higher c_hi means the agent grows bored faster from repeated failure.
Setting them equal gives a symmetric U-shape. Setting c_lo < c_hi (e.g. the Asymmetric preset) models an agent who tolerates being too skilled but strongly dislikes failing. The reverse models one who fears mastery more than failure.
